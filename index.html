About the Handbook

This book presents a comprehensive exploration of next-generation AI architectures for climate resilience, focusing on robust hazard prediction and efficient mitigation through advanced data-driven approaches. It details the use of large-scale, heterogeneous environmental datasets—including satellite imagery, GIS, hydrological and meteorological time- series, and real-time sensor data—alongside state-of-the-art preprocessing, feature engineering, and data fusion techniques.

The technical core covers cutting-edge machine learning frameworks such as deep convolutional neural networks for spatial analysis, recurrent networks and transformers for temporal modeling, and ensemble learning for improved prediction reliability. Special emphasis is placed on spatiotemporal modeling, attention mechanisms for interpretability, and hybrid architectures that integrate physical process models with data-driven AI. The integration of AI with cloud platforms like Google Earth Engine enables scalable, near real-time hazard monitoring and automated early warning systems.

The book highlights explainable AI (XAI) methods—such as SHAP and Grad-CAM—for transparent decision support in disaster management. Case studies showcase AI applications in wildfire detection, flood forecasting, drought monitoring, and landslide risk assessment, with validation against simulation models and ground truth. It further examines AI-enabled sensor networks, edge computing, and emerging technologies like generative AI for synthetic data, digital twins for environment simulation, and quantum computing for accelerating climate models, serving as a vital resource for researchers and practitioners advancing AI-based climate hazard solutions.

This upcoming book is being submitted to Elsevier for publication and Scopus indexing, ensuring high academic visibility and global reach.

Potential Chapter Titles

Part I: Foundations and AI Technologies (Why This Matters + Data Stack)

Chapter 1: Climate Hazards and Decision Contexts

Understanding the impacts of climate-related hazards on users, informing decision-making processes considering stakeholders and uncertainties, and framing choices through cost–loss analysis to optimize risk management strategies.

Chapter 2: Environmental Data for AI (Reserved)

Utilizes diverse environmental data sources like satellite imagery, GIS, hydro-meteorological time series, crowd-sourced inputs, QA/QC, and data fusion for comprehensive, real-time environmental analysis, climate modeling, disaster prediction, and sustainable resource management.

Chapter 3: Spatiotemporal AI/ML Basics

Involves using models like CNNs, transformers, and GNNs to capture spatial and temporal dependencies; integrating physics-informed ML hybrids to embed scientific knowledge; and employing rigorous evaluation techniques to ensure accuracy and reliability in environmental and climate data modeling.

Part II: Modelling Hazards (How We Predict)

Chapter 4: Geospatial AI with Remote Sensing

Advanced AI models for detection, segmentation, and change detection in satellite imagery, enabling precise, real-time monitoring of environmental and urban dynamics to inform disaster response, resource management, and urban planning.

Chapter 5:Time-Series & Sequence Models for Weather–Climate (Reserved)

Applications use RNNs and transformers to capture temporal dependencies, model climate regimes, and predict extremes, enabling improved forecasting of dynamic weather patterns and climate variability with enhanced accuracy and interpretability.

Chapter 6: Flood Forecasting & Basin Modelling

Combines hydrologic coupling with atmospheric data and hydrodynamic models to simulate flood events and generate inundation maps, enabling accurate prediction of flood extent and timing for effective risk management and response.

Chapter 7: Drought Monitoring & Early Warning

Integrates climate, hydrological, and environmental data with advanced analytics to detect drought onset and severity in real time, enabling timely, informed actions to reduce vulnerability and enhance resilience across affected communities and sectors.

Chapter 8: Wildfire Detection & Spread Prediction (Reserved)

Leverages satellite imagery, AI models, and impact-based forecasting to rapidly detect fires and accurately predict their spread, enabling timely and effective wildfire management and risk mitigation across large areas.

Chapter 9: Landslide Susceptibility & Triggering

Prediction combines ensemble AI/ML models with rainfall threshold analysis to accurately identify and forecast high-risk zones, enhancing early warning and disaster mitigation efforts.

Chapter 10: Cyclones, Storms, and Extreme Events

Events forecasting combines real-time tracking of storm paths and intensities with impact-based models to deliver early warnings; recent advances integrate AI and satellite data to improve accuracy, enabling better preparedness and risk reduction amid evolving climate patterns.

Part III: From Models to Operations (How It Runs)

Chapter 11: Uncertainty Quantification & Reliability

Involves measuring how well AI models estimate uncertainty (calibration), combining multiple models to improve robustness (ensembles), and making predictions interpretable and trustworthy through explainability techniques.

Chapter12: Real-Time Decision Support and Early Warning Systems

Integrates continuous monitoring, defined triggers, thresholds, alerting, and human-in-the-loop oversight to translate dynamic environmental data into timely, adaptive actions that mitigate climate risks and enhance resilience under uncertainty.

Chapter 13: Cloud & Edge for Environmental AI(Reserved)

Leverages cloud geoprocessing platforms like Google Earth Engine (GEE) and on-device edge computing to enable scalable, real-time, energy-efficient environmental data analysis, reducing latency and bandwidth while enhancing resilience and sustainability in monitoring and response.

Chapter 14: MLOps for Climate Systems

Ensures effective management of the ML lifecycle through data versioning, drift detection, continuous monitoring, and reproducibility, enabling reliable and scalable AI-driven climate solutions.

Chapter 15: Logistics & Humanitarian Operations

Affirms timely pre-positioning, efficient routing, and resilient supply chains to maintain critical aid delivery and operational continuity during climate-related disasters, enabling rapid, flexible responses that save lives and sustain communities through proactive planning and adaptive management.

Part IV: People, Policy, and Place (Where It Lands)

Chapter 16: Governance, Ethics, and Data Policy

Addresses governance frameworks, ethical considerations, and data policies vital for AI-driven climate resilience, emphasizing explainable AI (XAI) to foster trust, protect privacy, and ensure equitable outcomes in climate adaptation and mitigation efforts.

Chapter 17: Regional & Community Implementations

Illustrates how AI supports climate resilience in developing countries by enabling regional collaboration, cross-border early warning systems, and engaging local communities through citizen science for inclusive and effective adaptation strategies.
